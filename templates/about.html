The Philosophers Blog

The Philosophers Blog is a new and open forum for Philosophy in the twenty-first century.

Our articles dust down the old as well as new philosophical thoughts and bring them into fresh conversation with modern-day readers of all ages. We are fascinated by and passionate about all philosophical topics and wish to introduce as many people as possible to its thrills and its spills, its charms and its challenges.

Philosophy is an enormously rich and varied discipline: we are committed to sharing, in an intelligent and accessible form, the joys of ancient languages, literature, history, philosophy and religion.

The Philosophers Blog is an experiment in open learning. We wish to debate complex issues with balance and nuance. We encourage feedback and constructive criticism from our readers, as well as ideas for articles that will continue the conversation. In particular, we welcome possible answers to questions that we cannot answer ourselves. Our aim is always to encourage curiosity, foster discussion and find our collective way through the labyrinth of ideas, without knowing where exactly we will end up.

Most importantly, the contributors to The Philosophers Blog are united by a love of this discipline. To be sure, not every idea from philosophy deserves to be defended, and we enthusiastically invite critical analysis of those that may be wrong. On the whole, however, our writers do seek to uphold and promote ideals that held sway thousands of years ago: open enquiry, robust debate and the unfettered exploration of ideas.
